Ahwahnee (ah-WAA-nee) is the land of the Ahwahneechee (ah-wah-NEE-chee) tribe, a tribe of the Miwok (me-wUk) people. California was home to the Miwok people long before the white man stepped foot onto their land. In October of 1890 the Abraham Lincoln of the United States government passed a law; stealing the land from the Ahwahneechee people. The Ahwahneechee tribe were a hunter gather tribe; the men spent most of their time hunting rabbits and squirrels and making tools. Obsidian, or volcanic class, was a hot commodity within the tribes; it originated from the east side of the Sierra Nevada. Obsidian was used for weapons and tools and was traded amongst the neighboring tribes. Women spent a lot of time preparing food and weaving baskets.They would use dogwood, a type of shrub or small tree, to weave. In colder months the dogwood turns more of a red tint giving the baskets that same red color. Within the village there were different types of buildings of different shapes and sizes. Their homes where called “U’macha”, meaning ‘bark home’, made out of, you guessed it, bark. They also have a structure called a Roundhouse, this is where traditional ceremonies are held.

Descendants of the Ahwahneechee tribe have fought long and hard to regain at least some rights to the land that was originally theirs. Thankfully. Yosemite National Park now allows the indigenous people to use the Roundhouse in the Ahwahneechee Village located within the park for religious and traditional ceremonies. For about 250 years, the indigenous tribes on the west coast remained untouched and unknown to the new commers. It wasn’t until the California Gold Rush when the native tribes of Ahwahnee started having conflict with the white men who invaded their land to look for gold. Violent battles broke out between the gold seekers and the indigenous people. Once Ahwahnee was claimed by the US Government, the indigenous people weren’t pushed out immediately, they were still and essential part of the economy at the time. As time went on, the tribe became more and more oppressed until they no longer inhabited their own land.
